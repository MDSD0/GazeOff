A practical 20-20-20 reminder for Windows.

The 20-20-20 routine is simple: after roughly 20 minutes of near-screen work, spend at least 20 seconds looking at something around 20 feet away. GazeOff turns that idea into a quiet Windows timer that can fit around real work.

How GazeOff applies the routine

GazeOff starts with a 20-minute work interval and a 25-second short break. The extra five seconds gives you time to settle your focus beyond the screen instead of racing a timer. After several short breaks, GazeOff schedules a longer recovery break. Every interval and duration remains adjustable.

Work interval
20 minutes by default
Eye break
25 seconds by default
Long recovery
3 minutes after five short cycles
Availability
Windows 10 and Windows 11

Reminders that know when to wait

A rigid timer can interrupt a presentation, meeting, film, or game at exactly the wrong moment. Smart Pause uses local Windows signals to hold the timer during calls, fullscreen activity, video playback, and time away. No screen recording, audio recording, account, or cloud activity dashboard is involved.

More than a break timer

Optional blink reminders and posture nudges cover two habits that a basic 20-20-20 timer misses. Local recovery statistics help you see whether breaks are becoming consistent without sending your activity to an online account.
