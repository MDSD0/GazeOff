An eye break reminder designed for Windows work.

GazeOff lives in the Windows system tray and schedules short distance-focus breaks before a long screen session becomes uncomfortable. It stays close enough to help and quiet enough to leave your desktop alone.

What a useful Windows break app should handle

A break reminder needs more than a repeating alarm. It should pause when you step away, avoid interrupting meetings and fullscreen activity, and return without demanding attention. GazeOff combines a customizable break timer with local idle, call, video, and fullscreen detection.

Automatic away breaks

When you leave the computer for several minutes, GazeOff pauses the work timer and counts the time away as a completed break. Closing a laptop lid or putting Windows to sleep also holds the timer until the device becomes active again. That prevents overnight inactivity from producing a pile of false reminders.

Calm reminders, not productivity surveillance

GazeOff tracks local recovery statistics so you can understand your own screen habits. It does not record your screen, audio, video, documents, or browsing history. There is no cloud dashboard and no account to maintain.
